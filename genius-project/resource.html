Further Research Opportunities

If you're interested in further research about feline intelligence, here are some places to get started.

Articles & Online Reports

  • "Cats rival dogs on many tests of social smarts. But is anyone brave enough to study them?" by David Grimm
    • Primary article used in research for this website. Talks about the history of feline (and canine) intelligence studies, and tests undergone by cats.
  • "In what ways are cats intelligent?" by Denyse O'Leary
    • Compares feline and canine intelligence in multiple ways, or rather, argues how there is no single one-size-fits-all means of comparing feline and canine intelligence due to unique species differences and histories.
  • "How intelligent are cats?"
    • A shorter and briefer article addressing similar topics to the previous link, with intriguing bonus information about how cats were actually once briefly used as messengers like pigeons.
  • "Cats really do need their humans, even if they don't show it" by Sarah Jackson
    • This article goes a little deeper into Vitale's experiments concerning the bonds of cats with their owners, as mentioned in the gallery.

Videos & Documentaries

  • Inside the Mind of a Cat, 2022
    • A documentary that came out on Netflix. It gives a brief history of the cat-human relationship and adresses many questions of feline intelligence, such as whether cats know their own names, play favorites, or actually love their owners.
  • "Which are smarter, cats or dogs? We asked a scientist" by PBS NewsHour
    • A brief video lasting a mere three minutes. I admittedly ahve not watched this because despite the three minutes I do not have unlimited time.

Books & Physical Materials

  • How Smart is My Cat? by David Alderton
    • This book explains some of the possibly mystifying behaviors of cats, as well as providing several simple tests the reader can use to determine the relative IQs of their own cats.
  • The Intelligent Cat by Grace Pond and Angela Sayer
    • This book is quite similar to the above, providing another series of simple memory tests cat owners can use on their own pets.
